# Welcome to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to your comprehensive guide to **Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics**! This textbook is designed to take you from foundational concepts to advanced implementations in the exciting field of embodied artificial intelligence.

## üéØ What You'll Learn

This course covers the complete spectrum of physical AI and humanoid robotics:

- **Foundations**: Understanding what makes AI "physical" and why it matters
- **Robotics Fundamentals**: Core principles of robot design, sensors, and actuators
- **Humanoid Systems**: Bipedal locomotion, balance, and human-like movement
- **Perception**: Computer vision, sensor fusion, and environmental understanding
- **Control & Planning**: Motion planning, trajectory optimization, and intelligent control
- **Machine Learning**: Deep learning, reinforcement learning, and sim-to-real transfer
- **ROS 2**: Industry-standard robotics middleware and ecosystem
- **Simulation**: Gazebo, Isaac Sim, and physics-based virtual environments
- **Manipulation**: Grasping, force control, and dexterous manipulation
- **Human Interaction**: Natural interfaces, safety, and ethical considerations
- **Advanced Topics**: Swarm robotics, soft robotics, and emerging technologies
- **Hands-On Projects**: Build real robotic systems from scratch

## üìö Course Structure

The textbook is organized into **12 comprehensive chapters**, each building on previous knowledge:

### Part I: Foundations (Chapters 1-3)
Learn the fundamentals of physical AI, robotics principles, and humanoid robot design.

### Part II: Core Systems (Chapters 4-6)
Master perception systems, motion planning, and machine learning for robotics.

### Part III: Tools & Platforms (Chapters 7-8)
Work with ROS 2 and modern simulation environments.

### Part IV: Applications (Chapters 9-10)
Explore manipulation, grasping, and human-robot interaction.

### Part V: Advanced & Practical (Chapters 11-12)
Discover cutting-edge topics and build complete robotic systems.
